Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe is a landlocked country in southern Africa, bordered by South Africa, Botswana, Mozambique, and Zambia, and is best known for its natural wonders like Victoria Falls and its ancient heritage at Great Zimbabwe, the ruins of a medieval stone city. With Harare as its capital and around 16 million people speaking 16 official languages, Zimbabwe is culturally diverse and resilient. Formerly called Rhodesia, it gained independence in 1980 under Robert Mugabe. Today, its economy relies on agriculture and mining, while sadza, a maize-based porridge, remains the staple food. Despite political and economic challenges, Zimbabweans are celebrated for their optimism, hospitality, and strong sense of community.
